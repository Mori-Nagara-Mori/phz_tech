Understanding the Issue

Symptoms

You may encounter situations where your printer is not turning on after the switch is flipped to ON.

Considerations

To apply the correct solution, you must first identify whether this is a power-related or a touch panel-related issue.

Here are some scenarios that you should consider to analyze the situation better:

  1. If the sensor light is glowing when the printer is turned on, it suggests that it is not a power issue but more than likely a touch panel issue.
  2. If the mainboard indicators light up when the printer is turned on after removing the front cover, it suggests that it is not a power issue but more than likely a touch panel issue.
  3. If none of those lights are visible when the printer is turned on, you should look into its power components and the mainboard using the suggestions below.

Approaches

Check the Power Cord Output Voltage

How to do it?

Watch the tutorial here.

  1. Prepare your multimeter for the AC range.
  2. Plug the cord into an electric outlet.
  3. Measure the output voltage of the cord.
  4. The correct output voltage should be 110~240 V AC depending on your country or region.

What to do next?

  1. The voltage is between 100 and 240 V AC Move on checking the fuse
  2. The voltage is not between 100 and 240 V AC The power cord is broken, and other components are unknown

Check the Fuse

How to do it?

Watch the tutorial here.

  1. Make sure the printer is turned off and the power source is disconnected.
  2. Use a slotted screwdriver or a hex key to pry and remove the fuse from the power switch.
  3. Set your multimeter to test continuity or audible continuity.
  4. A functional fuse should have a reading close to 0 when performing a continuity test or create a loud beep noise when performing an audible continuity test.

What to do next?

  1. The voltage is between 100 and 240 V AC Move on checking the fuse
  2. The voltage is not between 100 and 240 V AC The power cord is broken, and other components are unknown

Check the Power Switch Output Voltage

How to do it?

Watch the tutorial here.

  1. Make sure the printer is turned off and the power source is disconnected.
  2. Take out the power switch after removing the 2 flathead screws using an H2 hex key.
  3. Disconnect the two highlighted wires from the switcher.
  4. Set your multimeter to measure the AC range.
  5. Plug the power cord into the switch socket and measure the two pins from the disconnected wires.
  6. The correct output voltage should be 110~240 V AC depending on your country or region.

What to do next?

  1. The voltage is between 100 and 240 V AC Move on checking the PSU
  2. The voltage is not between 100 and 240 V AC The power switch is broken and other components are unknown

Check the Power Supply Unit (PSU) Output Voltage

How to do it?

Watch the tutorial here.

  1. Remove the front case of the printer.
  2. Plug in the power cord and turn on the printer.
  3. Set your multimeter to measure the DC range.
  4. Measure the input voltage of mainboard terminal 1.
  5. The correct input voltage of the mainboard should be 24 V.

What to do next?

  1. The voltage is 24 V DC Check the mainboard input voltage
  2. The voltage is not 24 V DC The PSU broke and the mainboard is unknown
